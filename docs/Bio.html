Rita Giordano

I am a physicist turned into a data scientist and I am currently based in UK. Life brought me travelling all across Europe. From the highest volcano in Europe, where I graduated in Physics, I landed to the French Alps to do a PhD at the European Synchrotron Radiation Facility (ESRF) in statistics and physics applied to structural biology. After my PhD, from Alps to Alps, I did my first postdoc at the Swiss Light Source (SLS). Later on I moved to the capital of Europe to discover beautiful vineyards and to start to work as biostatistician. Never tired to travel and discover new places, I decided to come back to academic research and I started a second post-doc in one of the oldest university in Europe, Cambridge. After this great experience I continued my European tour and after a parental leave I began my data scientist adventure in Karlsruhe. Afterwards I came back to Strasbourg to start a new life as data scientist freelance. Lastly I moved again to Cambridge where I worked a as a data scientist for the Royal Society of Chemistry and now I seek a new challenge in data visualisation.

I like to create visualisations and learning new stuff day by day. Please feel free to visit the section “Portfolio” to see some examples of what I do and I love to do.
